Automobiles are another major cause of atmospheric pollution in metro cities. To prevent the emission of poisonous gases
from automobiles, catalytic converters, having expensive metals namely platinum-palladium and rhodium as the catalysts
are fitted into automobiles. Use of unleaded petrol or diesel can reduce toxic emissions. The Air (Prevention and Control
of Pollution) Act came into force in 1981 in India. It was amended in 1987 to include noise as an air pollutant.
