11.2.2: Cloning vectors
Vector is any DNA molecule that carries a gene of interest to be inserted into the host organism. For example, plasmid.
Plasmid
is an autonomously replicating extrachromosomal genetic content present in the bacteria. It is different from the
chromosomal DNA. It is used as a vehicle for transfer of gene of interest into the host cell. Plasmid contain origin of
replication, site where replication begins when gene of interest enters the host cell. It also contains antibiotic resistance
gene.
