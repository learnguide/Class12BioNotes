Devices for cross pollination
1.
Dicliny:
There are two types of flowers, male and female. The plants may be monoecious or dioecious.
2.
Dichogamy
: Anther and stigma mature at different times.
(i) Protandry
: Anthers mature earlier. E.g,
Salvia, Clerodendron
, sunflower, rose
(ii) Protogyny:
Stigmas mature earlier. E.g.
Plantago, Magnolia, Mirabilis
3.
Self-sterility:
Pollen grains are incapable of growing over the stigma of the same flower e.g. Tobacco,
some crucifers. The ability of the pollen grain to grow faster on the stigma of another plant than of the
same is called
prepotency
( e.g. apple)
4.
Heterostyly:
Within the flowers the styles and the stamens are at different heights
