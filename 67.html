Chromosomal disorders
When disorders are caused due to absence or excess or abnormal arrangement of one or more chromosomes. Improper
segregation of chromatids during cell division cycle results in the gain or loss of a chromosome(s), called aneuploidy. For
example, Down’s syndrome results in the gain of extra copy of chromosome 21. Similarly, Turner’s syndrome results due
to loss of an X chromosome in human females. Absence of cytokinesis after telophase stage of cell division results in an
increase in a whole set of chromosomes in an organism and, this phenomenon is known as polyploidy. This condition is
often seen in plants.
