In Angiosperms, the zygote is formed, inside the ovule. After fertilization, the sepals, petals and stamens of
the flower fall off. The pistil, however, remains attached to the plant.
•
In plants:
a.
Zygote develops into embryo.
b.
Ovule develops into seed
c.
Integument of the ovule develops into seed coat.
d.
Ovary develops into fruit.
e.
Ovary wall develops into pericarp,
is protective in function.
After dispersal, seeds germinate under favorable condition to produce new plants.
