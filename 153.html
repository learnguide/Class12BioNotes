PATTERNS OF BIODIVERSITY LATITUDINAL AND ALTITUDINAL GRADIENTS
(i) Latitudinal gradient
•
There is little biodiversity at the poles. Biodiversity is more in temperate areas but is maximum in tropical rain
forests. This is because the tropical rain forests have more favourable conditions for more varieties of organisms
and with less or no catastrophes. This has allowed for stability in the environment allowing more species to
develop.
•
In temperate areas harsh conditions exist only during the cold season. However, very harsh conditions prevail
for most of the year in arctic regions.
•
Number of vascular species is 118-236/0.1 ha in tropical forests and 21-48 species 0.1 ha in temperate forests.
Their number would 10/0.1 ha in arctic regions
.
(ii) Altitudinal gradient
•
A decrease in species diversity occurs as we ascend high mountains due to drop in temperature and greater
seasonal variability.

•
German geographer and naturalist Alexander von Humboldt observed that species richness within a region
increases with increasing area but only up to a certain limit.
•
The relationship between species richness and area is a rectangular hyperbola for a wide variety of taxa whether
they are birds, bats, fresh water fishes or flowering plants.
•
On a logarithmic scale it is a straight line.
