Microsporogenesis
•
The formation and differentiation of microspore is called
microsporogenesis
.
•
The PMCs undergo meiosis. Each forms tetrahedral tetrads.
•
Cytokinesis may be successive or simultaneous.
•
Tetrad are of five types, tetrahedral, isobilateral, decussate, T shaped, linear. Tetrahedral is most
common.
•
In successive type, the cell wall is formed after meiosis
–
I as well as meiosis
–
II forming an isobilateral
pollen tetrad. It is a characteristic feature of monocot.
•
In simultaneous type, each nuclear division in microspore mother cell is followed by cell wall
formation.
