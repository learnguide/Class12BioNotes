Inheritance of one gene
Inheritance of one gene can be explained using Mendel one of the hybridization experiment such as crossing between tall
and dwarf plants. When the tall and dwarf plants are crossed, the resulting first hybrid generation is known as Filial
progeny or first-generation progeny. Mendel observed that all the plants formed are tall. Then the F1 plants were self-
pollinated and produce F2 generation. The progeny obtained has three tall plants and one dwarf. The resulting ratio is 3:1.
